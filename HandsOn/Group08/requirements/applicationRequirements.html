Proposed application: BCN Mobility & Air Explorer

Web app integrating air quality, traffic by segments, and Bicing stations to (i) explore city status, (ii) compare areas, and (iii) suggest micromobility routes through less congested areas with better air quality.

Users and goals

  • Citizens using Bicing: find nearby stations and healthier routes.
  • Data journalism / research: analyze traffic–pollution correlation.
  • City administration: identify critical segments for action.

Functional requirements

  • FR1. Base map with layers: Bicing stations, traffic segments, air quality stations.
  • FR2. Filters by date/time and by neighborhood/district.
  • FR3. Detail panel on click (indicators, basic time series).
  • FR4. “Healthier route” search between two points (avoiding dense/very dense segments and prioritizing zones with lower NO₂/PM10).
  • FR5. Export results to CSV and GeoJSON.

Non-functional requirements

  • NFR1. Data and metadata will be published as RDF using vocabularies: schema.org (place), SOSA/SSN (observations), Time Ontology, DCAT (catalog), and GeoSPARQL.
  • NFR2. External links: districts/municipal boundaries (open cartography), Wikidata for geographic entities.
  • NFR3. Licenses preserved in metadata (dcterms:license).
  • NFR4. Accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA) and a public SPARQL API.

RDF modeling and linking plan

  • Air quality observations → sosa:Observation with sosa:observedProperty (NO₂, O₃, PM10), time:Instant, and sosa:hasFeatureOfInterest the station (schema:Place + geo:Point).
  • Traffic segments → schema:Route/geo:Feature, status as sosa:Result or skos:Concept (very fluid… congestion).
  • Bicing stations → schema:CivicStructure/schema:BicycleParking with capacity and type.
  • External links: Barcelona districts on Wikidata (QIDs), municipal cartography, GeoNames, etc.
