Application Requirements — MoviSalud Madrid

1. Summary

The proposed application, MoviSalud Madrid, integrates three open datasets — BiciMAD stations, Air Quality monitoring, and Traffic Accidents — to support healthier and safer mobility decisions in Madrid. The app will show citizens where to find the nearest bike station, the air quality conditions in that area, and the historical accident risk, producing a combined MoviSalud Index to recommend the best travel options.

2. Scope

  • In: Visualization of bike stations, air quality stations, and accident hotspots; calculation of the MoviSalud Index; map and tabular exploration; export of results.
  • Out: Real-time routing, user authentication, mobile native app development, integration of private mobility data.

3. Actors & Use Cases

  • Actor: Citizen looking for a healthy and safe biking option.
  • UC1: Search for available bike stations nearby and check the MoviSalud Index of each option.
  • UC2: Visualize air quality and accident density per district.
  • UC3: Compare districts based on health and safety indicators.
  • UC4: Export selected data (CSV/GeoJSON) for further analysis.

4. Functional Requirements (FR)

  1. FR1: Visualize a map combining BiciMAD bike stations and air quality stations.
  2. FR2: Show a heatmap of traffic accidents, filterable by year, month, day, and time.
  3. FR3: Compute a MoviSalud Index for each district/cell, weighting air quality, accident data, and bike availability.
  4. FR4: Suggest the closest bike station with the best MoviSalud Index within a given radius.
  5. FR5: Export results in CSV or GeoJSON format and optionally provide a SPARQL endpoint for RDF queries.

5. Non-Functional Requirements (NFR)

  • NFR1: Data normalized into RDF using standard vocabularies (SOSA/SSN, WGS84, Schema.org, Time, GeoSPARQL optional).
  • NFR2: Code and data versioned in GitHub; optional CI for RDF validation (ShEx/SHACL).
  • NFR3: Respect license conditions and always cite data source and update date.
  • NFR4: Web app accessible (WCAG AA compliance) and responsive for desktop/tablet/mobile.

6. Data Requirements

  • BiciMAD dataset: Station ID, name, latitude, longitude, available bikes, docks.
  • Air Quality dataset: Station ID, coordinates, pollutant concentrations (NO₂, PM₁₀, O₃), timestamp.
  • Traffic Accidents dataset: Accident ID, date, time, district, location (coordinates), number of fatalities.
  • Linking strategy: Join on geographic proximity (lat/lon within buffer, or district attribute).

8. Acceptance Criteria

  • FR1: Given BiciMAD and air quality datasets, when the map is loaded, then both types of stations appear with distinct markers.
  • FR2: Given 10 years of accident data, when the user filters by year=2023, then only accidents of 2023 are displayed in the heatmap.
  • FR3: Given pollutant and accident data, when the MoviSalud Index is computed, then values are normalized (0–100) and displayed per district.
  • FR4: Given a user location, when searching within 1 km radius, then the system returns the closest BiciMAD station with the best MoviSalud Index.
